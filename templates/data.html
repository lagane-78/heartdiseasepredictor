Training and testing data

We trained and tested the heart disease prediction models on the Cleveland Heart Disease dataset from the UCI Machine Learning Repository. This data is one of few publicly available sources with necessary fields to develope heart and cardiovascular disease prediction models. The data includes 76 attributes in total, but we use 14 primary variables that may include both those that are more highly available indicators for patients, and are likely to have predictive power.

  • Age (years):
  • Sex (1=male, 0=female):
  • Chest pain (1=typical angina, 2=atypical angina, 3=non-anginal, 4=none):
  • Resting blood pressure (mmHg):
  • Cholesterol (mg/dl):
  • Fasting blood sugar:
  • Resting ECG (0=normal, 1=ST-T wave abnormality, 2=LVH):
  • Max heart rate:
  • Exercise induced angina (1=yes, 0=no):
  • Peak exercise (1=upsloaping, 2=flat, 3=downsloping):
  • Number of blood vessels colored by flouroscopy:
  • Thalassemia (3=normal, 6=fixed defect, 7=reversible defect):
